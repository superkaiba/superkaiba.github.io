About

I am a PhD student in computer science co-supervised by Yoshua Bengio and Guillaume Lajoie at Mila and Université de Montréal.

I am also currently an AI research fellow at Anthropic, working on mechanistic interpretability of emergently misaligned models.

My research has been funded by a Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarship, NSERC Canada Graduate Scholarship, and FRQNT Scholarship.

In my free time, I enjoy traveling, watching/analyzing good movies, and writing sad songs (check out my amateur music here)!

Reach out at thomas.jiralerspong@mila.quebec if there is anything you want to discuss, I'm always happy to talk!

Interests

I have a wide array of research interests, most of which I have been fortunate enough to work on:

  • LLMs/VLMs: LLM agents, LLM internals and mechanistic interpretability, LLM applications
  • Neuroscience/Cognitive Science/Psychology Inspired AI: Input-driven learning, bias-only learning
  • Modularity: Compositionality, discrete representations, discovering discrete subgoals
  • Model-based AI: Causality (causal graph discovery, causal imputation), model-based reinforcement learning
  • AI for Good: AI for healthcare (mechanical ventilation), AI for climate change (HVAC control), AI for autonomous driving, AI for drug discovery
