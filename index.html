About

I am a PhD student at Mila and Université de Montréal, supervised by Yoshua Bengio and Guillaume Lajoie. Currently an AI Research Fellow at Anthropic. I have a wide variety of research interests (see below for a list of my publications and experience), but I have most recently been working on mechanistic interpretability, compositionality, and neuroscience-inspired AI.

I previously worked at Occam AI (LLM agent optimization), Waabi (autonomous driving simulation), and conducted research with Prof. Blake Richards (neuroscience-inspired RL) and Prof. Doina Precup (model-based RL). My work has been supported by the Vanier CGS, NSERC CGS, and FRQNT.

I am always happy to chat, collaborate, or mentor. Feel free to reach out at thomas.jiralerspong@mila.quebec.

Selected First Author Publications

  • Cross-Architecture Model Diffing With Crosscoders Under Review

    We show that sparse autoencoders can be used to discover behavioral differences between language models with different architectures in an unsupervised manner

  • Learning What Matters: Steering Diffusion via Spectrally Anisotropic Forward Noise Under Review

    We show that controlling frequency components in the forward diffusion process improves generative quality and enables better compositional learning

  • A Complexity-Based Theory of Compositionality ICML 2025

    We develop a theoretical framework connecting algorithmic complexity with compositional learning, showing when and why neural networks learn compositional representations

  • Geometric Signatures of Compositionality Across a Language Model's Lifetime ACL 2025 · Highlight Award

    We identify geometric signatures in LLM representations that predict compositional generalization and track how these emerge during training

  • Expressivity of Neural Networks with Random Weights and Learned Biases ICLR 2025

    We prove that neural networks can learn complex functions using only bias parameters while keeping weights random, characterizing the expressivity of this architecture

  • Shaping Inductive Bias in Diffusion Models through Frequency-Based Noise Control DeLTa Workshop 2025

    We demonstrate how manipulating frequency components of noise in diffusion models shapes their inductive biases toward specific types of structure

  • Efficient Causal Graph Discovery Using Large Language Models ICLR Workshop 2024

    We show that LLMs can efficiently discover causal relationships from observational data by leveraging their world knowledge to guide structure learning

  • Towards Safe Mechanical Ventilation Treatment Using Deep Offline RL AAAI 2023

    We develop a deep offline RL approach for learning safe ventilator control policies from ICU data, with safety constraints to prevent harmful actions

  • Forecaster: Towards Temporally Abstract Tree-Search Planning from Pixels NeurIPS Workshop 2023

    We introduce a model-based RL method with affordance-aware tree-search planning that operates directly from pixel observations

  • Deep Conservative RL for Personalization of Mechanical Ventilation Treatment RLDM 2022

    We apply conservative offline RL to personalize mechanical ventilation treatment in critical care, addressing distributional shift in medical settings
